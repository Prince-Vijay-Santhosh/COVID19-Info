COVID - 19

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus.

Most people infected with the virus will experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and recover without requiring special treatment. However, some will become seriously ill and require medical attention. Older people and those with underlying medical conditions like cardiovascular disease, diabetes, chronic respiratory disease, or cancer are more likely to develop serious illness. Anyone can get sick with COVID-19 and become seriously ill or die at any age.

The best way to prevent and slow down transmission is to be well informed about the disease and how the virus spreads. Protect yourself and others from infection by staying at least 1 metre apart from others, wearing a properly fitted mask, and washing your hands or using an alcohol-based rub frequently. Get vaccinated when it’s your turn and follow local guidance.

The virus can spread from an infected person’s mouth or nose in small liquid particles when they cough, sneeze, speak, sing or breathe. These particles range from larger respiratory droplets to smaller aerosols. It is important to practice respiratory etiquette, for example by coughing into a flexed elbow, and to stay home and self-isolate until you recover if you feel unwell.

Symptom's of Covid-19

People may experience different symptoms from COVID-19. Symptoms usually begin 5–6 days after exposure and last 1–14 days. But the most common symptoms are:
Fever

Body temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher, or feeling cold.
A sudden onset of fever is one of the most common symptoms of COVID-19. It is important to note that not everyone with COVID-19 will experience a fever.

Chills

Feeling cold, even when you are in a warm environment.
Chills can be a sign that your body is trying to fight off an infection.
They can also be a side effect of some medications, such as fever reducers.

Sore throat

Pain or scratchiness in the throat.
A sore throat is a common symptom of many respiratory illnesses, including COVID-19.
It can also be caused by allergies, dry air, or smoking.

Covid-19 Q/A

Precautions

To prevent the spread of COVID-19:

1) avoid crowds and keep a safe distance from others, even if they don’t appear to be sick;
2) wear a properly fitted mask if you feel sick, have been close to people who are sick, if you are at high-risk, or in crowded or poorly ventilated areas;
3) clean your hands frequently with alcohol-based hand rub or soap and water;
4) cover your mouth and nose with a bent elbow or tissue when you cough or sneeze;
5) dispose of used tissues right away and clean your hands; and
6) if you develop symptoms or test positive for COVID-19, self-isolate until you recover.

COVID-19 vaccines:

COVID-19 vaccines work by teaching your body's immune system to fight off the virus. They do this by introducing a weakened or inactive form of the virus to your body. Your immune system then produces antibodies that can fight off the virus if you are later exposed to it. 

There are many different COVID-19 vaccines available, and they work in different ways. Some vaccines use a technology called messenger RNA (mRNA) to teach your body's immune system how to fight the virus. Other vaccines use a technology called vector vaccines to deliver the virus to your body.

COVID-19 vaccines are safe and effective. They have been rigorously tested and have been shown to be very effective at preventing serious illness, hospitalization, and death from COVID-19.

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommends that everyone 6 months of age and older get vaccinated against COVID-19. This includes people who have already had COVID-19.
